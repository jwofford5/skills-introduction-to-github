## Recommended Visual Element 1 — Model Comparison Table

**Placement:** After "Comparative Analysis" subsection in Theoretical Models section

**Caption:** *Table 1.* Comparison of three principal emotional intelligence models across key dimensions.

**Alt-text:** A four-column table comparing the ability model, mixed model, and trait model of emotional intelligence on the dimensions of conceptual basis, primary theorists, measurement approach, primary instrument, and psychometric standing.

**Content:**

| Dimension | Ability Model | Mixed Model | Trait Model |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Conceptual basis** | Cognitive ability | Ability + personality + motivation | Personality disposition |
| **Primary theorists** | Mayer, Salovey, Caruso | Goleman; Bar-On | Petrides, Furnham |
| **Measurement approach** | Performance-based | Self-report / multi-rater | Self-report |
| **Primary instrument** | MSCEIT | EQ-i; ECI | TEIQue |
| **Psychometric standing** | Most rigorous; lower overlap with personality | High practitioner uptake; higher personality overlap | Transparent personality framing; good trait validity |
| **Incremental validity** | Modest but significant above g and Big Five | Lower incremental validity | Moderate; overlaps with Neuroticism/Agreeableness |

*Note.* MSCEIT = Mayer-Salovey-Caruso Emotional Intelligence Test; EQ-i = Bar-On Emotional Quotient Inventory; ECI = Emotional Competence Inventory; TEIQue = Trait Emotional Intelligence Questionnaire; g = general cognitive ability.

## Recommended Visual Element 2 — Four-Branch Model Diagram

**Placement:** Within "The Ability Model" subsection

**Caption:** *Figure 1.* The four-branch model of emotional intelligence (Mayer, Salovey, & Caruso, 2004), arranged from foundational to integrative capacities.

**Alt-text:** A vertical hierarchy diagram showing four branches of emotional intelligence. From bottom to top: Branch 1 (Perceiving Emotions), Branch 2 (Using Emotions to Facilitate Thought), Branch 3 (Understanding Emotions), Branch 4 (Managing Emotions).
